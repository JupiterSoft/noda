# Who Can Use Noda?

Noda can be useful for people and teams that need AI systems to answer from approved knowledge.

It is designed for knowledge that can be organized into short canonical articles.

## SaaS Teams

SaaS teams can use Noda for:

* product helpdesk;
* onboarding;
* internal support;
* documentation;
* pricing and subscription explanations;
* support automation.

## Experts and Consultants

Experts and consultants can use Noda to organize repeated explanations and client-facing knowledge.

Examples:

* consulting notes;
* training materials;
* client FAQ;
* expert knowledge base;
* private AI assistant;
* paid or free knowledge access.

## Legal and Compliance Teams

Legal and compliance teams can use Noda to organize internal explanations, procedures, approved answers, and client-facing materials.

Noda does not provide legal advice by itself. The responsibility for legal content remains with the lawyer, legal team, or organization using Noda.

## Education and Training

Educators can use Noda for:

* course knowledge;
* student FAQ;
* onboarding materials;
* learning support;
* AI tutor knowledge base;
* internal teaching notes.

## Internal Teams

Companies can use Noda for:

* internal procedures;
* employee onboarding;
* team documentation;
* operational knowledge;
* internal AI assistants.

## AI Builders

Developers and AI teams can use Noda as a structured knowledge layer for:

* AI agents;
* MCP-connected tools;
* AI helpdesk bots;
* support automation;
* product documentation;
* knowledge experiments.

## When Noda Is a Good Fit

Noda is a good fit when:

* the same questions appear repeatedly;
* answers should come from approved knowledge;
* knowledge needs to be updated without retraining a model;
* access should be limited by category;
* AI clients need to search and read articles.

## When Noda May Not Be the Right Fit

Noda may not be the right fit if:

* you only need a general chatbot;
* you do not want to prepare structured knowledge;
* your knowledge cannot be maintained as articles;
* you need a marketplace, CRM, or payment system;
* you expect the model to generate all answers without a knowledge base.

## Summary

Noda is useful for teams that want predictable AI answers based on structured knowledge.

It works best when important knowledge can be turned into short, clear, reusable articles.
