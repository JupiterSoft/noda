# Commercial Knowledge Service

Noda can be used for private, internal, shared, or paid knowledge services.

Noda does not define the user's business model.

The user decides who gets access, why they get access, and under what conditions.

## Possible Uses

Noda may be used for:

* private knowledge management;
* internal team knowledge;
* client support;
* education;
* consulting;
* expert knowledge;
* onboarding;
* documentation;
* AI helpdesk workflows;
* paid knowledge services.

## What Noda Provides

Noda provides the technical platform for storing, organizing, and accessing knowledge.

This may include:

* tenant workspace;
* articles;
* categories;
* tags;
* access keys;
* OAuth/MCP connection;
* API-style access;
* allowed operations;
* category-based access control;
* search and article retrieval;
* subscription and usage limits.

## What the User Controls

The user controls the external relationship.

This includes:

* who gets access;
* whether access is free or paid;
* what the price is;
* how users or clients are managed;
* how payments are handled outside Noda;
* contracts;
* invoices;
* taxes;
* promises and obligations to users.

## What Noda Does Not Do

Noda is not:

* a marketplace;
* a payment system for the user's clients;
* a reseller of the user's knowledge;
* a publisher of the user's content;
* a broker between the user and their clients.

## Technical Access Workflow

A typical access workflow:

1. Create and organize knowledge in Noda.
2. Separate knowledge by categories.
3. Create an access key or OAuth/MCP key.
4. Configure permissions:
   * Read;
   * Write, only if needed;
   * allowed operations;
   * allowed categories.
5. Give access only to authorized users, AI clients, applications, or systems.

## Summary

Noda provides the technical platform and access-control tools.

The user's business model, clients, pricing, payments, contracts, taxes, and obligations remain the user's responsibility.
