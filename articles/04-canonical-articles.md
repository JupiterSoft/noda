# Canonical Articles

Noda is based on the idea of short canonical articles.

A canonical article is a small reusable piece of knowledge that answers one question or explains one stable concept.

## Why Canonical Articles Matter

AI systems need clear sources of truth.

Large documents often contain mixed topics, old information, examples, notes, and incomplete ideas. This makes it harder for AI to know what should be used in an answer.

A canonical article is easier to trust, search, update, and reuse.

## Good Article Shape

A good Noda article usually has:

* one clear title;
* one main topic;
* short paragraphs;
* stable facts;
* clear limits;
* practical examples, if needed;
* no unrelated material.

## Example Topics

Good canonical article topics:

* What is Noda?
* How to create an access key?
* How to connect Noda to ChatGPT?
* What is the Personal Plan?
* Can I sell access to knowledge stored in Noda?
* How to provide access to knowledge in Noda?
* What happens after a refund?

## Improving AI Answers

When an AI helpdesk gives a weak answer, the problem is often visible:

missing article
unclear article
wrong category
insufficient tags
bad retrieval

This is easier to debug than a vague prompt problem.

Instead of rewriting a large system prompt, the team can add or improve the article.

## Summary

Canonical articles make knowledge easier for AI systems to search, read, and use.

Noda encourages the pattern:

one article = one stable piece of knowledge
